Domestic Critical Materials Refining from Electronic Waste DOE Application Briefing & Execution Plan

4LCORP is pursuing a domestic critical-materials refining platform that uses electronic waste as a strategic feedstock to help reduce U.S. dependence on foreign processing capacity, with an initial focus on tungsten and cobalt.

The Opportunity

The United States has a refining bottleneck, not a mining problem. China controls 75% of global critical mineral refining and 90% of rare earth processing. The U.S. government has committed tens of billions in bipartisan, legislatively locked funding to build domestic capacity. The gap is in refining — turning raw e-waste and mineral feedstock into usable separated metals. That is where the value is, where the money is flowing, and where no dominant domestic player exists.

4LCORP's Position

Track 1 — Now

Electronics procurement & distribution serving government and military clients. Provides cash flow and direct feedstock visibility into the e-waste streams Washington wants recovered domestically.

Track 2 — The Strategic Play

Domestic critical materials refining using e-waste as strategic feedstock. Multi-metal hydrometallurgical recovery. The position no one else occupies: feedstock access + refining capability.

Existing capabilities: Active refining operations in Indonesia and Africa recovering 8 metals (6 on 2025 USGS Critical Minerals List). Academic partnership with Texas A&M University for process optimization and next-gen recovery. CMMC certification creating a 12–18 month compliance barrier for defense e-waste containing CUI.
